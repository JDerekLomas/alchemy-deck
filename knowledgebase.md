# Alchemy Deck - Source Reference

Sourced quotes from [Source Library](https://sourcelibrary.org) for the 34 oracle cards.

## Stages

**Nigredo** - "I am black, white, yellow, and red." —Maier, *Atalanta Fugiens* (1618), p. 150

**Albedo** - "Water washes and purifies all imperfect things which are not fixed." —Sendivogius, *New Chemical Light* (1628), p. 150

**Citrinitas** - "On the high summit of the mountain stands the vulture constantly crying: I am white and black, yellow and red." —Maier, *Atalanta Fugiens*, Emblem XLIII

**Rubedo** - "The fixed becomes volatile; the volatile, fixed; and a new World is established." —Khunrath, *Amphitheater* (1609), p. 306

## Operations

**Calcination** - "All impurities and pollutions of things are purified by fire. Every compound is dissolved by fire." —Sendivogius, *New Chemical Light*, p. 150

**Dissolution** - "Water joins together everything that is dissolved." —Sendivogius, *New Chemical Light*, p. 150

**Separation** - "Fire separates everything that is joined, and that which is of its own nature and property it optimally purifies." —Sendivogius, *New Chemical Light*, p. 150

**Conjunction** - "In the mixture of these things—purified on the surface—remember to observe the triple secret of composition... otherwise the animated spirit cannot be joined to the body." —Khunrath, *Amphitheater*, p. 306

**Fermentation** - "Nature gives motion, motion excites air, air excites fire, and fire separates, purifies, digests, colors, and causes all seed to mature." —Sendivogius, *New Chemical Light*, p. 150

**Distillation** - "The tincture represents the Soul, the spirit of wine represents the Body and spirit: nor can they ever be separated." —Drebbel, *Two Treatises* (1628), p. 58

**Coagulation** - "Dissolve your metal or mineral, without losing any of its spirits, in distilled vinegar: let it slowly coagulate in a warm place." —Drebbel, *Two Treatises*, p. 59

## Elements

**Fire** - "Fire acts in wonderful hidden ways upon other Elements and upon all things." —Sendivogius, *New Chemical Light*, p. 150

**Water** - "Water washes and purifies all imperfect things." —Sendivogius, *New Chemical Light*, p. 150

**Air** - "Motion excites air, air excites fire." —Sendivogius, *New Chemical Light*, p. 150

**Earth** - "Earth receives the Tincture." —Sendivogius, *New Chemical Light*, p. 150

**Quintessence** - "The Quintessence is an eternal, immutable, incombustible thing, like the invincible heaven, perfect in all the elements." —Drebbel, *Two Treatises*, p. 45

## Principles

**Sulphur** - "Fire, Sulphur; Salt, Balsam; Mercury—the three called Prima Materia, having only one name, like God." —Paracelsus, *Book of Meteors* (1566), p. 14

**Mercury** - "From living quicksilver comes the prima materia: the proximate universal matter." —*Rosicrucian MS* (17th c.), p. 85

**Salt** - "The Sun is fire, salt, and base." —Paracelsus, *Book of Meteors*, p. 14

## Vessels

**Athanor** - "With one Athanor furnace; and one fire." —Khunrath, *Amphitheater*, p. 306

**Alembic** - "Extract a tincture from the dregs or material remaining in the bottom of the cucurbit." —Drebbel, *Two Treatises*, p. 60

**Crucible** - "The body is corrupted, destroyed, dies, turns black, and is reduced to ashes. Do not value this ash lightly, O son, for it is the diadem of your body." —Khunrath, *Amphitheater*, p. 306

**Retort** - "It always begins there where it ends." —Hermes, *Pymander* (1532), p. 10

## Sages

**Hermes** - "The artisan mind, together with the word, containing the circles, and swiftly rotating them with rapid motion, bent its own mechanism towards itself." —*Pymander*, p. 10

**Paracelsus** - "These three are Prima Materia, having only one name, like God, namely Materia Prima. And just as in the Godhead there are three Persons." —*Book of Meteors*, p. 13

**Boehme** - "Heaven is the Firmament, the Sea of Fire, from the 7 Spirits of Nature, from which the Stars as a Quintessence, with the Word 'Fiat' were formed." —*Aurora* (1612), p. 144

**Ficino** - "Hermes Trismegistus, wisest of all Egyptians, attempted to eradicate this wicked stain from the souls of men." —*On Pleasure* (1497), p. 13

**Sendivogius** - "Fire acts in wonderful hidden ways upon other Elements and upon all things. For just as the soul is from that purest essence, so the vital principle is from the Elemental." —*New Chemical Light*, p. 150

**Agrippa** - "God is primarily the end and origin of all virtues, impressing the seal of Ideas upon His ministering Intelligences." —*Three Books of Occult Philosophy* (1550), p. 50

## Arcana

**Prima Materia** - "The Prima Materia is a divine mist, issuing from God's Paradise from the East, pouring itself into a great quantity of heavenly waters, and coagulating." —Hellwig, *Curious Physics* (1714), p. 53

**Lapis** - "He is the powerful one, praised by Hermes in the Emerald Tablet. He is the true living Mercurius... because in him is the living fire." —Hellwig, *Curious Physics*, p. 31

**Ouroboros** - "It always begins there where it ends." —Hermes, *Pymander*, p. 10

**Rebis** - "Man bore the image of his father, for God delighted in his own form." —*Pymander*, p. 10

**Elixir** - "The tincture... healing all diseases to a miracle, and endowed with whatever properties previously mentioned. For the tincture represents the Soul." —Drebbel, *Two Treatises*, p. 58
